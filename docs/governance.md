# Governance & Community Files

Files that establish community standards and support structures. Especially important for large or growing projects.

## CODE_OF_CONDUCT

Defines expected behavior for community members. **Highly recommended** for projects with multiple contributors.

### Required Contents
- Expected standards of behavior
- Unacceptable behavior definitions
- Reporting procedures for violations
- Enforcement responsibilities
- Consequences for violations
- Scope of application

### Standard Recommendation
The Contributor Covenant is the industry-standard code of conduct template widely recognized and adopted by major projects.

### Why It Matters
- Sets clear expectations for behavior
- Provides enforcement guidelines
- Makes community welcoming and safe
- Often required by hosting platforms
- Prevents conflicts before they escalate

### When to Include
- Highly recommended for large projects or organizations
- Recommended for projects seeking contributors
- Recommended for any project with a community
- Advisable even for smaller projects

### Naming Variants
- `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`
- `CODE_OF_CONDUCT`
- `CONDUCT.md`
- `CONDUCT`
- `CoC.md`

## SUPPORT

Explains how users can get help with the project.

### Required Contents
- Where to ask questions (forums, chat platforms, mailing lists)
- How to report bugs
- Expected response times
- Paid support options (if available)
- Self-help resources and documentation links
- Community channels
- Contact information for maintainers

### Naming Variants
- `SUPPORT.md`
- `SUPPORT`
- `HELP.md`
- `HELP`
- `CONTACT.md`
- `CONTACT`
- `TROUBLESHOOTING.md`
- `TROUBLESHOOTING`

### Key Information to Provide
- Distinction between bugs and support questions
- Available support channels
- Response time expectations
- Community guidelines for support requests

## GOVERNANCE

Describes how decisions are made in the project. **Critical for large projects and organizations.**

### Required Contents
- Decision-making process and procedures
- Roles and responsibilities definitions
- Voting procedures (if applicable)
- Process for becoming a maintainer or core team member
- Conflict resolution procedures
- Amendment process for governance itself
- Meeting schedules and procedures (if applicable)
- Technical steering committee structure (if applicable)

### When to Include
- Highly recommended for organizations (not just single projects)
- Essential for very large projects
- Recommended for projects with multiple maintainers
- Not necessary for small personal projects

### Naming Variants
- `GOVERNANCE.md`
- `GOVERNANCE`
- `CHARTER.md`
- `CHARTER`
- `DECISIONS.md`
- `DECISIONS`

### Key Elements
- Clear hierarchy and authority structure
- Transparent decision-making processes
- Defined paths for community participation
- Succession planning for leadership roles

## Best Practices

1. **Start with CODE_OF_CONDUCT early** - Don't wait until problems arise
2. **Use Contributor Covenant** - It's well-tested and widely recognized
3. **Make SUPPORT accessible** - Link prominently from README
4. **GOVERNANCE prevents conflicts** - Define processes before disputes arise
5. **Be realistic** - Don't create governance overhead you can't maintain
6. **Update regularly** - Review and update as project grows
7. **Be transparent** - Make all governance documents easily discoverable
